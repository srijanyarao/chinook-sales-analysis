
📊 Revenue Overview
Total Revenue
The Chinook store generated $2,328.60 in total revenue across all recorded transactions.

Revenue by Year
Revenue remained stable across the 5‑year period:
- Highest: 2010 — $481.45
- Lowest: 2009 — $449.46
- Variation between years is minimal, indicating consistent customer engagement.
Revenue by Month
Monthly revenue reveals more detailed patterns:
- 2009: Extremely stable — nearly identical revenue every month
- 2010–2012: Moderate variation with occasional spikes
- 2013: Most volatile year, with both the lowest and one of the highest monthly values
These patterns suggest a mix of stable baseline purchasing behavior with occasional periods of increased demand.

🎵 Top‑Selling Products Analysis
Top Tracks
The highest‑earning tracks are primarily TV show episodes, such as Pilot, Gay Witch Hunt, and Phyllis’s Wedding. Each of these tracks generated multiple purchases, indicating strong customer demand for episodic content. This suggests opportunities for bundling episodes or promoting full seasons.
Top Genres
Rock is the dominant genre, contributing over 35% of total revenue. Latin, Metal, and Alternative & Punk also perform strongly, reflecting a customer base with diverse but rock‑leaning preferences. TV Shows appear in the top 5 genres, reinforcing the trend that episodic content drives meaningful revenue.

🎤 Top Artists by Revenue — Insights
The revenue distribution across artists reveals several strong patterns:
1. Rock and Metal dominate the catalog
Artists like Iron Maiden, U2, Metallica, and Led Zeppelin generate the highest revenue. This aligns with the genre‑level analysis, confirming that rock is the store’s strongest revenue driver.
2. TV show soundtracks perform surprisingly well
Titles associated with Lost, The Office, and Battlestar Galactica appear in the top revenue list. This suggests strong customer interest in episodic or soundtrack content.
3. Brazilian artists show strong performance
Artists such as Os Paralamas Do Sucesso, Chico Buarque, Tim Maia, and Gilberto Gil generate meaningful revenue. This indicates a strong Brazilian customer base or a well‑performing regional catalog.
4. Revenue follows a long‑tail distribution
A small number of artists generate a large share of revenue, while many artists have only one or two purchases. This is typical of digital media platforms and highlights opportunities for targeted recommendations.
5. The catalog supports diverse musical tastes
While rock dominates, the presence of jazz, classical, blues, and world music artists in the revenue list shows that the store appeals to a wide range of listeners.
